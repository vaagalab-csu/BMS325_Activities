Quiz — Mechanism, prediction, and analysis

1) You apply a Ca²⁺ channel blocker and the presynaptic AP is still present. Explain why the postsynaptic current disappears. Include all the causal steps through receptor activation.

2) What is the effect of blocking EAAT transport?

A. Narrower glutamate transient, shorter AMPA and NMDA currents.
B. Prolonged glutamate transient, prolonged AMPA and NMDA currents (especially NMDA tail).
C. AP repolarization slows because EAATs remove extracellular K⁺.

3) You hold Vpost at −70 mV vs −40 mV. What happens to AMPA vs NMDA currents, and why?

4) A student claims: “NMDA current is delayed only because glutamate arrives later.” Is that claim correct? Choose best answer.

A. Correct — NMDA receptors bind glutamate only after AMPA receptors.
B. Incorrect — NMDA kinetics are slower and voltage-dependent (Mg²⁺ block), even with the same glutamate transient.
C. Correct — NMDA receptors require presynaptic Ca²⁺ entry to open.

5) Design an experiment (in one paragraph) using two pharmacological manipulations to distinguish “release failure” from “postsynaptic receptor block.”

6) BoNT/TeNT is applied. Which traces remain and which vanish?

A. AP and Ca²⁺ current remain; glutamate and postsynaptic currents vanish.
B. Only NMDA current vanishes; AMPA current remains.
C. All traces vanish because BoNT blocks Na⁺ channels.

7) If you see a normal glutamate transient but no AMPA current at any holding potential, what is the most parsimonious explanation?

A. AMPAR antagonist or AMPAR nonfunctional/absent (postsynaptic).
B. TTX is present.
C. EAATs are blocked.

8) At Vpost=+20 mV, the simulator shows outward glutamatergic currents. Explain why the sign flips, and what remains voltage-dependent.

Helpful hints

Your answer is marked “complete” if it includes the key mechanistic elements below.
  • Causal chain: AP → VGCC opening → Ca²⁺ entry → vesicle fusion → glutamate transient → receptor activation.
  • Release dependence: transmitter release requires presynaptic Ca²⁺ and SNARE-mediated fusion.
  • Driving force: I = G·(V−Erev), with Erev ~ 0 mV for glutamatergic currents.
